About Me

I have always had a passion for computer science which started from editing the HTML code for my MySpace page and blossomed into installing Linux on my computer at a middle school age. My Bachelor's degree was in Medical Laboratory Science where I found a love for analytics and a desire for a job which engaged my problem solving skills more than the lab could do. My Master's degree has taught me programming languages such as Python, SQL, and R and machine learning techniques in Python. I have also used Tableau and Qlik for data visualization. Even though my Master's degree has ended, my educational journey never ends. I am currently learning on Codecademy.com where I have become familiar with HTML and JavaScipt.

Riding my bicycle throughout any city I am in, cooking with new ingredients, and film photography. All pictures besides the one below have been taken on my 1975 Minolta camera.

Previous Work

I have been a Medical Laboratory Scientist since graduating with my Bachelor's in 2017. I am looking to switch careers to data science with my recent graduate degree.

During my career in the lab, I have completed other analytical tasks on top of my regular daily work. For example, I compiled years worth of quality incident reports and categorized each incident in Excel. After that, I analyzed the trends and determined which incidents did not need a separate report written, because the incident information was already in the Laboratory Information System (LIS). I also was responsible for monitoring turn-around-time data for test results in patients who were suspected of having a stroke. These were very high priority test results and needed to be resulted within 30 minutes of receipt in the lab.

Current Work

My final Capstone project for my Master's is an indepth analysis of health, income, and education data for Allegheny County. All of the datasets were sourced by myself and needed extensive feature engineering before applying 6 different classification models to the data to determine which factors most influence food desert status.

Assignments and projects that I have completed during my Master's degree can be found on my GitHub.

Most projects on my GitHub are written in Python in a Jupyter Notebook, with this exception of this website. I have become very familiar with Python packages Pandas, Numpy, Scikit-Learn, and matplotlib. Machine learning is used in many of these projects, implemented using scikit-learn. I have experience running multiple grid searches, neural networks, decision trees, and logisitic regression to name a few.

Education is a lifelong journey, as such I am currently learning on Codecademy. As well as developing my programming skills by doing practice problems on Leetcode.
